Dear Siblings,
You are not alone.

About Sib-for-Sibs

We are a community of individuals in Washington D.C., who have siblings on the Autism spectrum, connecting with others like us. Each individual with autism is unique, but many of the challenges and triumphs they and their siblings experience are the same. By connecting siblings in the D.C. area, we are hoping to create an environment in which they feel comfortable sharing their experiences, and can support other siblings in need.

Mission of Support, Growth and Learning

It’s our Mission to enhance the lives of Families affected by Autism, through community support and helpful resources. Supporting and learning from each other is the basis of our network and the foundation to improving our relationships with our Autistic siblings. You’re not alone; we’re in this together!

Who we are

The network was founded by Ariella Grosse and Kyle Mann, who both have a sibling on the autism spectrum.They were connected through Autism Speaks Executive Director Marley Rave, and quickly became friends and committed to work together to connect other siblings in the D.C. Metro area.

I have always been very close to my brother, but not in the conventional way that my peers were close to their siblings. It wasn’t until I was well into my teenage years that I began to realize just how many other siblings there were who had a sibling on the autism spectrum. Learning about these other families helped me feel like a part of a larger community, and much less alone. I wish I had known much earlier about all the other siblings in this area. I want to help other siblings feel a sense of security and community knowing that there are other kids out there who share many of the same feelings and challenges.
-Ariella Grosse
Growing up, it was sometimes a challenge to understand and relate to my sister. Often I felt unable to share my feelings and frustrations with my peers. A sibling support network provides a sense of community, through learning about others’ unique perspective and experiences; while embracing differences and similarities to cultivate stronger relationships with our siblings. My sister has taught me the most important lessons – that it’s all about empathy, unconditional love, and support.
-Kyle Mann
